1
Prepare the Stock Solution (0.01 M)

Sodium oleate has a molar mass of 304.4 g mol⁻¹. To make 100 mL of 0.01 M solution:

n = C × V = 0.01 mol/L × 0.100 L = 1.0 × 10⁻³ mol
m = n × M = 1.0 × 10⁻³ × 304.4 = 0.304 g

Accurately weigh 0.304 g of sodium oleate, transfer to a 100 mL volumetric flask, add ~60 mL distilled water, swirl gently (warm to 30–40 °C if needed), cool to room temperature, then make up to the 100 mL calibration mark and mix thoroughly.

2
Prepare Solution B — 0.001 M

Using the dilution equation C₁V₁ = C₂V₂:

V₁ = (C₂ × V₂) / C₁ = (0.001 × 100) / 0.01 = 10.0 mL

Pipette 10.0 mL of Solution A into a clean 100 mL volumetric flask. Make up to the mark with distilled water and mix. This is a 1:10 dilution — concentration decreases by one order of magnitude.

3
Prepare Solution C — 1×10⁻⁴ M

Using the dilution equation C₁V₁ = C₂V₂:

V₁ = (1×10⁻⁴ × 100) / 0.001 = 10.0 mL

Pipette 10.0 mL of Solution B into a clean 100 mL volumetric flask. Make up to the mark with distilled water and mix. Each 1:10 dilution step reduces the concentration by one decade on the concentration scale.

4
Prepare Solution D — 1×10⁻⁵ M

Using the dilution equation C₁V₁ = C₂V₂:

V₁ = (1×10⁻⁵ × 100) / 1×10⁻⁴ = 10.0 mL

Pipette 10.0 mL of Solution C into a clean 100 mL volumetric flask. Make up to the mark with distilled water and mix. The final series spans 0.01 M → 1×10⁻⁵ M, covering four decades above and below the CMC of sodium oleate (~1.6–2.1 × 10⁻³ M).
